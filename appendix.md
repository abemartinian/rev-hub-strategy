# Appendix A: Illustrative Operating Views for Rev-Hub

This appendix outlines a set of familiar operating views and illustrative signals that could support Rev‑Hub. These views reflect how sales leadership already inspects and runs the business today. They are intended to provide specificity and ground discussion, not to prescribe final metrics, definitions, or build requirements. Rev‑Hub is not intended to surface all signals at once. These views represent different lenses leaders move between depending on the decision at hand.

---

## A1. Baseline Performance: Are We Pacing to Plan?

**Purpose:** Provide a consistent, shared baseline view of revenue and pipeline so leadership starts from the same facts before diagnosing issues or making tradeoffs.

| Signal | What Leadership Uses It For | Type |
|--------|----------------------------|------|
| ARR/eARR by region, segment, and motion (new logo vs. expansion) | Understand where revenue is coming from and where growth is concentrated (by product) | Lagging |
| Actuals vs. plan (current quarter and YTD) | Assess pacing against targets before acting | Lagging |
| Partner registered deals &amp; service bookings | Understand where partners are already contributing to current bookings | Lagging |
| Pipeline coverage ratio | Quickly identify quarters or regions at risk | Leading / Lagging |
| Pipeline stage distribution | Spot bottlenecks and late-stage risk | Leading |
| Activity pacing indicators (meetings, calls) | Detect behavior gaps before they impact outcomes | Leading |
| Win rate and deal velocity trends | Distinguish between volume problems and execution problems | Leading / Lagging |
| Forecast vs. commit | Understand the gap between what the field is calling and what the model projects | Leading / Lagging |
| New pipeline created (WoW, MoM, QoQ) | Detect early whether pipeline generation is keeping pace with consumption | Leading |
| ARR by product (with MoM and WoW trend) | Track which products are driving growth and where momentum is accelerating or stalling | Lagging |

## A2. Opportunity Gaps: Where Are We Leaving Money on the Table?

**Purpose:** Surface mismatches between opportunity and execution that indicate where leadership attention or investment could unlock additional growth.

| Signal | What Leadership Uses It For | Type |
|--------|----------------------------|------|
| TAM vs. current penetration | Identify under-penetrated markets with long-term upside | Leading |
| Quota capacity vs. headcount by region / segment | Identify structural under-coverage before it appears in pipeline | Leading |
| Ramp-adjusted capacity | Understand true near-term selling capacity | Leading |
| Growth rate vs. coverage | Detect markets where demand outpaces execution capacity | Leading / Lagging |
| Partner attach rate | Surface underutilized ecosystem leverage | Leading |
| Renewal and expansion ARR exposure | Protect capital-efficient growth early | Leading |
| Customer scorecard | Understand customer health and churn risk to protect expansion and renewals | Leading / Lagging |
| New hire ramp tracker | Track whether new capacity is converting to productive quota contribution on schedule | Leading |

**Purpose:** Close the loop between leadership priorities and outcomes early enough to course correct — not after the quarter is closed. In FY26, these strategic bets are anchored in the Growth Levers defined in the Revenue Operating Plan.

| Field Simplicity &amp; Productivity | Leading activity or workflow adoption signal | Assess whether simplification bets are improving execution | Leading |
| CS, Microsoft &amp; Partners | Partner attach rate movement | Track whether ecosystem-led growth is scaling | Leading |

## A4. Operating Discipline: Are We Running the Business with Rigor?

**Purpose:** Ensure Rev‑Hub reinforces leadership inspection rhythms, not just reporting.

| Leadership Cadence | Signals Anchoring the Conversation | Owner | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Weekly pipeline review | Pipeline coverage, stage mix, near-term risk flags | VP Sales + Regional Sr. Directors | Every Monday, 30 min |
| Monthly performance review | Actuals vs. plan, leading indicators by region | Sales Leadership + Finance | First Tuesday monthly |
| Quarterly strategy review | Bet vs. outcome trends, course correction signals | Sales VPs + Sr. Directors + RevOps | End of quarter |

**Key questions per cadence:**

**Weekly:** Is pipeline coverage on track? Are near-term deals at risk of slipping? Where is stage velocity slowing?

**Monthly:** Are we pacing to plan by region and segment? Where are leading indicators diverging from plan? What actions are needed before the next review?

**Quarterly:** Are FY26 growth bets working? Where do we course correct for next quarter? What strategic tradeoffs are required?

**Dashboard view:** <a>A4 · Operating discipline tab</a>
